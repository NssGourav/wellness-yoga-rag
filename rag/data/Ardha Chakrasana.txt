The standing backward bend involves arching the upper torso toward the rear.
This pose focuses on stretching the front of the body and chest.
It helps to tone the muscles in the shoulders and the arms.
The posture requires careful balancing while creating a gentle arc shape.
Anyone with wrist, arm, or shoulder injuries should avoid this position.
Individuals with medical conditions that make balance difficult should not attempt it.
