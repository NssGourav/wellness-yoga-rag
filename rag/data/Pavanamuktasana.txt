TITLE: Pavanamuktasana (Wind-Relieving Pose)
The wind-relieving pose involves lying on the back and hugging the knees to the chest.
It is designed to massage the digestive organs and help release trapped gas.
This stretch helps to loosen the muscles in the lower back and the hips.
It is a grounding movement that promotes a sense of physical ease.
Avoid this pose if you have had recent abdominal surgery or a hernia.
Individuals with neck injuries should keep their heads on the floor during the stretch.
