This posture is a standing bend where the upper body folds toward the legs.
It is designed to make the spine more supple and flexible over time.
The pose provides a deep stretch to all the muscles located along the back.
It is known for helping to refresh and invigorate the nervous system.
You should avoid this pose if you have chronic back or neck injuries.
People currently recovering from leg injuries should also refrain from this bend.
